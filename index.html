What is the Campus Rose Competition?

This is a semester-long project designed to bring nature into our daily lives. Every participant receives a free starter kit containing high-quality rose seeds and nutrient-rich soil.


Over the next few months, you'll nurture your plant, track its growth on this website, and finally showcase your work. It doesn't matter if you're a gardening pro or a complete beginner‚Äîit's all about the journey!

Gardener's Care Guide

Sunlight

Roses love the sun! Ensure your pot gets at least 6 hours of direct sunlight every day. A south-facing window is best.

Watering

Keep the soil moist but not soggy. Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. Avoid getting water on the leaves to prevent fungus.

Troubleshooting

Yellow leaves? You might be overwatering. Drooping? It needs more water. Visit the club room if you need help!

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join the competition?

Any student currently enrolled in Grades 8-12 at our high school. Teachers are welcome to grow for fun, but cannot win prizes.

Does it cost money?

No! It is completely free. The Garden Club provides the seeds, soil, and basic starter pots thanks to our school funding.

What if my rose doesn't bloom in time?

That's okay! Bring it anyway. You can still win the "Healthiest Growth" or "Best Log" awards. Nature is unpredictable.

Can I work in a team?

Yes, teams of up to 2 students are allowed to share one plant entry.

How do I upload photos?

Once you register, you will get a login link sent to your student email. You can upload photos directly from your phone.
